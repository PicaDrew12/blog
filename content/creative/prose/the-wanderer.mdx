# The Wanderer

The road stretched endlessly before her, each step a echo of countless others. She had forgotten why she left, but the leaving itself had become her reason for continuing.

The villages blurred together—the same smell of bread, the same questioning glances, the same kindness of strangers who sensed her restlessness. She moved through them like a ghost, present yet absent, touching nothing that could hold her.

It was only when she stumbled upon the old oak tree, twisted and gnarled by centuries of wind, that something shifted. In its steadfast presence, she found a question: not where am I going, but where do I belong?
